## First Moments: Grammar Separating (0 to 380,000 Years)

### The Planck Era (0 to 10⁻⁴³ seconds)

**Conditions**:
- Temperature: ~10³² K (incomprehensibly hot)
- Density: ~10⁹⁶ kg/m³ (all mass-energy compressed)
- Size: ~10⁻³⁵ m (smaller than any particle we know)

**What we know**: Almost nothing. Our physics breaks down.

**What we suspect**: Quantum gravity era
- Space-time itself fluctuating quantum-mechanically
- All forces unified into single "superforce"
- Grammar completely different from today

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Energy conjugating so rapidly that space-time structure not yet stable
- Grammar attempting to crystallize but conditions too extreme
- All forces = single property of energy at this scale

### Force Separation (10⁻⁴³ to 10⁻¹² seconds)

As energy cooled (temperature dropped), **forces separated out**:

**At ~10⁻³⁵ seconds** (GUT era):
- Gravity separates first
- Strong nuclear + Electroweak still unified
- Temperature: ~10²⁷ K

**At ~10⁻¹² seconds** (Electroweak transition):
- Strong nuclear separates
- Electromagnetic + Weak still unified
- Temperature: ~10¹⁵ K

**At ~10⁻¹² seconds** (Electroweak breaking):
- Electromagnetic and Weak separate
- Now four distinct forces
- Higgs field gives particles mass
- Temperature: ~10¹⁵ K

**Why forces separate**:

As temperature drops, **symmetries break**:
- High energy: All forces symmetric, indistinguishable
- Lower energy: Symmetry breaking → forces become distinct
- Like water freezing: Liquid has full rotational symmetry, ice crystal breaks it (picks specific orientations)

**In verb-coin terms**:
- Initially: Energy conjugating with one unified force property
- Cooling: Different force aspects become distinguishable
- Result: Four separate force properties (strong, EM, weak, gravity) with different strengths and ranges

**Grammar evolution**: As forces separate, new rules emerge for how energy can conjugate. Each force has different grammar.

### Inflation: Recursive Exponential Expansion (10⁻³⁶ to 10⁻³² seconds)

**The horizon problem**: 
- Universe extremely uniform (same temperature everywhere to 1 part in 100,000)
- But distant regions never in causal contact (light couldn't travel between them)
- How did they "agree" on same temperature?

**Solution**: Inflation—brief period of exponential expansion

**What happened**:
- Tiny region of space (~10⁻²⁶ m) had time to reach uniform temperature
- Then: Space itself expanded exponentially
- Expansion factor: At least 10²⁶ (possibly far more)
- Duration: ~10⁻³² seconds
- Result: This tiny uniform region became our entire observable universe

**Mechanism**:
- **Inflaton field** (hypothetical scalar field) in metastable high-energy state
- Acts like cosmological constant (vacuum energy that doesn't dilute as space expands)
- Drives exponential expansion: a(t) ~ e^(Ht) where H is Hubble parameter
- Eventually decays to lower energy state → inflation ends → energy released as particles

**Recursive amplification**:
- Small region → energy drives expansion
- More space → more vacuum energy (doesn't dilute!)
- More energy → even faster expansion
- **Positive feedback loop**: expansion → more energy → more expansion

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Inflaton field = energy in metastable potential state (face-0)
- High potential energy drives space expansion
- Expansion creates more space with same potential energy density
- Recursive loop: space → energy → more space → more energy
- Eventually: Field "rolls down" to lower energy (face-0 → face-1 transition)
- Kinetic energy released as particles (reheating)

**Why this matters**:
1. **Solves horizon problem**: Uniform region expanded to cosmic size
2. **Solves flatness problem**: Expansion stretches space to nearly flat geometry
3. **Amplifies quantum fluctuations**: Microscopic variations stretched to astronomical scales

### Quark Soup and Nucleosynthesis (10⁻⁶ seconds to 3 minutes)

**Quark-Gluon Plasma** (10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁵ seconds):
- Temperature: ~10¹² K (too hot for protons/neutrons)
- Quarks and gluons free (not confined in hadrons)
- Energy mostly kinetic (face-1), all particles relativistic

**Quark Confinement** (~10⁻⁵ seconds):
- Temperature drops below ~10¹² K
- Strong force binds quarks into hadrons
- Protons (uud) and neutrons (udd) form
- **Grammar emerges**: QCD (quantum chromodynamics) confinement
  - Quarks can't exist isolated
  - Always bound in groups of 2 (mesons) or 3 (baryons)
- **In verb-coin terms**: Quarks' verb-coins locked into standing patterns (mass)

**Matter-Antimatter Annihilation** (~1 second):
- Equal amounts of matter and antimatter initially
- As universe cools, they annihilate: proton + antiproton → photons
- **Tiny asymmetry** (CP violation): ~1 extra matter particle per billion pairs
- Annihilation eliminates balanced portion
- **Survivors**: ~1 in 10⁹ particles (all matter in universe today)
- Result: Universe transitions from matter-antimatter balanced to matter-dominated

**Why this asymmetry exists**: Grammar (CP violation in weak force) creates slight imbalance
- Specific decay processes favor matter over antimatter
- Tiny effect (~0.0000001% difference)
- But sufficient to ensure matter survives
- If perfect symmetry: Total annihilation → radiation only → no atoms, stars, us

**Big Bang Nucleosynthesis** (3 seconds to 3 minutes):
- Temperature: 10⁹ to 10⁷ K (cool enough for nuclei to survive)
- Protons and neutrons fuse into light nuclei
- **Products**: 
  - ~75% hydrogen (1 proton)
  - ~25% helium-4 (2 protons, 2 neutrons)
  - Trace amounts: deuterium (1p, 1n), helium-3 (2p, 1n), lithium-7 (3p, 4n)
- **No heavier elements yet**: Too hot, density too low for sustained fusion

**Why these ratios**:
- Neutron-proton ratio set by temperature and weak force decay rates
- Initially ~1:1 (thermal equilibrium)
- Neutrons decay: n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ (half-life 10 minutes)
- By 3 minutes: Ratio ~1:7 (1 neutron per 7 protons)
- All remaining neutrons captured into helium-4 (most stable light nucleus)
- Result: (2 neutrons captured) / (14 protons + 2 neutrons from capture) = 2/16 = 12.5% by number
- By mass (helium is 4× heavier): ~25% helium, 75% hydrogen

**Observation confirms**: Old stars, gas clouds show ~75% H, 25% He by mass ✓

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Cooling allows standing patterns (nuclei) to form from kinetic chaos
- Strong force (short-range) + EM force (repulsion) compete
- Grammar determines which nuclei stable (mostly H, He at this temperature/density)
- Heavier elements must wait for stars (higher temperatures required)

### Recombination: Atoms Form (380,000 Years)

**Before recombination**:
- Temperature: >3000 K
- Ionized plasma: nuclei and electrons separate
- Photons constantly scattering off free electrons
- Universe opaque (light can't travel far without scattering)

**At recombination**:
- Temperature drops to ~3000 K
- Electrons and nuclei combine into atoms
- Mostly neutral hydrogen (1 proton + 1 electron)
- Photons decouple (no longer scatter off atoms efficiently)
- Universe becomes transparent

**Why "recombination" is misnomer**: Electrons and nuclei weren't "combined" before—they never existed as atoms previously. First time atoms form. Better term: "combination" or "decoupling."

**Result**: 
- **Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)**: Photons from this era, now redshifted
- Originally ~3000 K (visible/infrared light)
- Now ~2.7 K (microwave radiation) due to cosmic expansion stretching wavelengths
- **Perfect blackbody spectrum** (exactly matches theoretical prediction) ✓
- **Temperature variations**: ±0.0001 K (1 part in 100,000)
  - These variations are the amplified quantum fluctuations from inflation
  - Map of early universe structure—seeds of galaxies

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Cooling allows electrons (face-1, kinetic) to be captured by nuclei
- EM attraction + quantum constraints → stable atomic orbitals (grammar)
- Atoms form (standing patterns with electrons in face-0 potential wells)
- Photons now travel freely (face-1, kinetic, no longer constantly scattering)

**Grammar emerges**: Atomic structure (quantum mechanics of hydrogen, helium)
- Electron energy levels
- Selection rules for transitions
- Same grammar that persists today

**This is the "last scattering surface"**—the oldest direct observation we can make of the universe. Before this: opaque plasma. After this: transparent atomic gas.

**Verb-coin interpretation**:

## First Stars and Galaxies: Light Returns (200 Million to 1 Billion Years)

### First Stars: Population III (200-400 Million Years)

**How stars form**:
1. Gas cloud (H + He) collapses under gravity
2. Compression heats gas (gravitational potential → kinetic energy)
3. Center reaches ~10⁷ K
4. Hydrogen fusion ignites: 4H → He + energy
5. Fusion pressure balances gravitational collapse
6. Stable star forms

**First stars were massive**:
- 100-1000 solar masses (Sun is 1 solar mass)
- Why so massive? No heavy elements to help gas cool
  - Heavy elements (C, O, Fe) radiate heat efficiently
  - Pure H/He gas cools slowly → larger clouds required for collapse
  - Larger clouds → more massive stars
- Very hot, very bright, very short-lived
- Lifespan: Only a few million years (compare Sun: 10 billion years)

**Death of first stars**:
- Fuse H → He → C → O → Ne → Mg → Si → Fe (in core)
- Iron is peak of binding energy curve (can't fuse further and release energy)
- Core collapses → supernova explosion
- Ejects heavy elements into space
- Leaves behind neutron star or black hole

**Significance**:
- **First heavy elements** created (carbon, oxygen, iron, etc.)
- Enriches interstellar gas
- Next generation of stars forms from enriched gas
- Can be smaller (heavy elements help cooling → smaller collapse masses)
- **We are made of this stardust**—all atoms heavier than helium forged in stars

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Gravity (force property of standing energy/mass) compresses gas
- Potential energy (face-0, gravitational) converts to kinetic (face-1, thermal motion)
- High temperature → nuclei overcome EM repulsion → fuse (strong force binds)
- Fusion: Standing energy (mass) partially converts to kinetic (photons, neutrinos)
  - 4 hydrogen (mass 4.032 u) → helium (mass 4.003 u)
  - Lost mass (0.029 u) → energy (E=mc²)
  - This energy creates radiation pressure (resists gravity)
- Balance: Gravity inward = radiation pressure outward → stable star

**Grammar**: Stellar structure equations
- Hydrostatic equilibrium (pressure gradient balances gravity)
- Energy transport (radiation, convection)
- Nuclear reaction rates (strong force, quantum tunneling)
- All emerge from force-space-time relationships

### Galaxy Formation (400 Million to 1 Billion Years)

**Hierarchical assembly**:
- Small dark matter halos form first
- Normal matter falls in, forms stars
- Small galaxies merge into larger ones
- Larger galaxies merge into still larger ones
- Process continues to present day

**Galaxy types emerge**:

**Spirals** (like Milky Way):
- Disk of stars, gas, dust
- Spiral arms (density waves, not solid structures)
- Ongoing star formation
- Rotation: Outer stars orbit galactic center
- Form from gas-rich mergers with some angular momentum

**Ellipticals**:
- Spheroidal/ellipsoidal shape
- Little gas or dust (mostly old stars)
- Minimal star formation
- Random stellar orbits (no organized rotation)
- Form from major mergers (collisions randomize orbits)

**Irregulars**:
- No organized structure
- Often smaller galaxies
- Active star formation
- Result of interactions or ongoing mergers

**Why these shapes**:
- **Rotation + gravity** → flattens to disk (spirals)
  - Angular momentum conserved
  - Matter settles to plane perpendicular to rotation axis
  - Minimizes energy for given angular momentum
- **Major mergers** → disrupts disk → randomizes orbits (ellipticals)
- **Interactions** → tidal forces → irregular shapes

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Gravity (dominant force at this scale) organizes matter
- Rotation (angular momentum conservation) creates structure
- Energy: Potential (gravitational) ↔ Kinetic (orbital motion)
- Stable configurations: Minimum energy for given angular momentum
- Grammar: Gravitational dynamics + conservation laws → galaxy morphology

### Supermassive Black Holes: Galactic Centers

**Almost every large galaxy has supermassive black hole at center**:
- Milky Way: ~4 million solar masses
- Some galaxies: Billions of solar masses
- Located at exact center

**How they form**: Still debated, but likely:
- Seed black holes from first massive stars
- Grow by accreting gas and merging with other black holes
- Coevolve with galaxy (black hole mass correlates with galaxy bulge mass)

**Active Galactic Nuclei** (AGN/quasars):
- Supermassive black hole actively feeding
- Accretion disk of infalling matter
- Extremely bright (outshines entire galaxy)
- Jets of particles ejected along rotation axis
- Most luminous objects in universe

**Why important**:
- Regulate galaxy evolution (feedback from jets heats gas, stops star formation)
- Trace cosmic history (distant quasars show early universe)
- Extreme physics laboratory (strong gravity, relativistic jets)

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Black hole: Extreme curvature of space-time (gravitational grammar)
- Event horizon: Point of no return (escape velocity exceeds light speed)
- Mass concentrated to point or ring (standing energy at extreme density)
- Accretion: Matter losing orbital energy (face-0 potential → face-1 radiation)
- Jets: Matter ejected along rotation axis (magnetic fields channel flow)

## Planetary Systems: Cosmic Nurseries (5-10 Billion Years Ago)

### Our Solar System Formation (4.6 Billion Years Ago)

**From nebula to system**:

**Step 1: Molecular cloud collapse**
- Region of giant molecular cloud begins collapsing
- Trigger: Nearby supernova shock wave or spontaneous instability
- Cloud: 99% hydrogen + helium, 1% heavier elements (from previous stars)

**Step 2: Disk formation**
- Cloud has slight rotation (angular momentum)
- Conservation of angular momentum: Spin speeds up as cloud contracts
- Flattens into protoplanetary disk (like pizza dough spinning)
- Center: Dense protostar
- Disk: Where planets will form

**Step 3: Sun ignites**
- Protostar core reaches ~10⁷ K
- Hydrogen fusion begins: 4H → He + energy
- Sun becomes main-sequence star (stable for ~10 billion years)

**Step 4: Planetary accretion**
- Dust grains in disk collide, stick together (electrostatic forces)
- Grow to pebbles, then rocks, then boulders
- Gravity becomes significant at ~1 km diameter (planetesimals)
- Planetesimals collide, merge into protoplanets
- Largest protoplanets gravitationally dominate, accrete remaining material
- Result: 8 planets, asteroid belt, Kuiper belt

**Step 5: Differentiation**
- Inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars):
  - Rocky (close to Sun, light elements evaporated)
  - Differentiated (heavy elements sank to core when molten)
- Outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune):
  - Gas/ice giants (far from Sun, light elements retained)
  - Large due to capturing hydrogen and helium gas
- **Frost line**: ~3-4 AU (where water ice can exist)
  - Inside: Only rock and metal survive
  - Outside: Ice, gas retained → larger planets

**Why this structure**:
- **Temperature gradient**: Hotter near Sun, cooler farther out
- **Composition gradient**: Heavy elements (rock, metal) everywhere, but light elements (H, He) only retained far from Sun
- **Accretion efficiency**: Outer disk has more mass (larger area), more material to accrete

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Cloud collapse: Gravitational potential (face-0) → kinetic (face-1 rotation, thermal)
- Disk formation: Angular momentum conservation (rotational grammar)
- Dust sticking: Electromagnetic forces (chemical bonding) at small scale
- Planetesimal growth: Gravitational accretion (force amplifies mass concentration)
- Differentiation: Heavy materials (more standing energy/mass) sink in gravity well

**Grammar**: Planetary system formation
- Conservation of angular momentum (from rotation symmetry)
- Temperature-dependent chemistry (evaporation/condensation)
- Gravitational dynamics (N-body accretion)
- All emerges from force-space-time relationships

### Exoplanets: Thousands of Other Systems

**Discovery methods**:
- Transit method (planet blocks some starlight)
- Radial velocity (star wobbles due to planet's gravity)
- Direct imaging (for large planets far from star)
- Gravitational microlensing (planet bends background starlight)

**What we've found** (~5000+ exoplanets confirmed):
- Planets around most stars
- Huge variety: Hot Jupiters, Super-Earths, Mini-Neptunes, Earth-analogs
- Many systems very different from ours
- Confirms: Planet formation is common outcome

**Habitable zone** (where liquid water possible):
- Not too close to star (too hot → water evaporates)
- Not too far (too cold → water freezes)
- For Sun-like star: ~0.95-1.37 AU
- Earth at 1 AU: Right in the middle
- Mars at 1.5 AU: On outer edge (cold, but once had liquid water)

**Implications**:
- Billions of potentially habitable planets in our galaxy alone
- Life may be common (but we don't know yet—sample size: 1)
- We're not special location (Copernican principle)
- But we're in right conditions for life (anthropic selection)

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Each detection method measures energy patterns revealing planets
- Transit: Standing mass (planet) blocks kinetic photons (starlight)
- Radial velocity: Planet's gravity creates Doppler shift in stellar spectrum
- Direct imaging: Photons from planet detected (reflected or thermal emission)
- Gravitational lensing: Planet's mass curves light paths
- All methods: Use face-1 (kinetic light/motion) to detect face-standing (planetary mass)

**Statistical significance**:
- ~5000+ confirmed exoplanets (as of 2024-2025)
- ~20-50% of Sun-like stars have Earth-sized planets in habitable zone
- Milky Way: ~100-400 billion stars → ~20-40 billion potentially habitable planets
- Observable universe: ~2 trillion galaxies → incomprehensible number of potential life sites

**This confirms framework prediction**: Planet formation is natural outcome of disk dynamics
- Gravity + angular momentum + dust accretion → planets form inevitably
- No special conditions required
- No design, just force-generated grammar operating in space-time
- Same process happening around stars throughout universe

## Why This Matters: From Cosmic to Local

**The universe created conditions for life through purely recursive processes**:

### Step 1: Create Heavy Elements

**Problem**: Big Bang only made H, He, trace Li
- No carbon (basis of organic chemistry)
- No oxygen (water, respiration)
- No nitrogen (proteins, DNA)
- No phosphorus (DNA backbone, ATP)
- No sulfur (proteins, metabolic cofactors)
- Life as we know it: Impossible without these

**Solution**: Stellar nucleosynthesis
- First generation stars (Population III):
  - Pure H/He fuel
  - Massive (100-1000 solar masses)
  - Fuse H → He → C → O → Ne → Mg → Si → Fe
  - Short-lived (few million years)
  - Die as supernovae
- Supernova explosion:
  - Ejects heavy elements into space
  - Creates elements heavier than Fe (through r-process, s-process)
  - Enriches interstellar medium
- Second generation stars:
  - Form from enriched gas
  - Contain ~1% heavy elements
  - Can have rocky planets
  - Our Sun: Second or third generation

**Timeline**:
- First stars: ~200 million years after Big Bang
- First supernovae: ~300 million years
- Galactic enrichment: Gradual over billions of years
- Our solar system: Formed 4.6 billion years ago from gas with ~2% heavy elements

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Stars: Convert standing energy (mass of nuclei) to kinetic (photons, neutrinos)
- Fusion releases energy: Standing → kinetic
- But also: Creates heavier nuclei (new standing patterns)
- Supernovae: Violent collapse releases enormous kinetic energy, forges heaviest elements
- Result: Universe recursively builds heavier elements over generations

**Without this recycling**: No carbon atoms in your body, no oxygen in air, no silicon in computers, no iron in blood. Life impossible.

### Step 2: Create Stable Stars

**Problem**: Need long-lived energy source
- Chemical reactions: Too weak, exhaust quickly
- Gravitational collapse: Provides initial heating but unsustainable
- Nuclear fusion: Perfect—enormous energy, billions of years

**Solution**: Main sequence stars like Sun
- Balance point: Gravity (inward) = radiation pressure (outward)
- Stable for 10 billion years (Sun is 4.6 billion years old, ~5 billion to go)
- Steady energy output (varies less than 0.1% over millions of years)
- Provides continuous illumination to planets

**Why stability crucial**:
- Life needs billions of years to evolve (Earth: 3.8 billion years so far)
- Requires steady energy input (can't adapt to rapid stellar changes)
- Habitable zone must remain stable (can't have star expanding/contracting wildly)

**Stellar lifetime depends on mass**:
- Massive stars (>8 solar masses): 
  - Very bright but short-lived (millions of years)
  - Planets don't have time for life to develop
- Medium stars (0.5-2 solar masses):
  - Moderate brightness, long-lived (billions to trillions of years)
  - Perfect for life evolution
- Small stars (<0.5 solar masses):
  - Dim but extremely long-lived (trillions of years)
  - Habitable zone very close to star (tidal locking issues)

**Our Sun**: Perfect mass (~1 solar mass) for stable, long-lived habitable zone

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Star equilibrium: Two forces balanced
  - Gravity (mass/standing energy attracts inward)
  - Radiation pressure (kinetic photons push outward)
- Stable balance: Negative feedback loop
  - Compress slightly → temperature rises → more fusion → more pressure → expansion
  - Expand slightly → temperature drops → less fusion → less pressure → compression
- Grammar: Stellar structure equations ensure stability

### Step 3: Create Planets in Habitable Zones

**Problem**: Need solid surface and liquid water
- Gas giants: No surface, too hot/cold
- Too close to star: Water evaporates
- Too far from star: Water freezes

**Solution**: Rocky planets in habitable zone
- Disk formation: Inevitable from rotating collapse
- Rocky planets: Form in inner disk (refractory materials survive near star)
- Habitable zone: Distance where liquid water stable
  - Sun: ~0.95-1.37 AU (Earth at 1 AU, perfect)
  - Depends on stellar brightness: Brighter stars → farther HZ

**Earth specifics**:
- Mass: Large enough to retain atmosphere (gravity holds gases)
- Not too large: Doesn't become gas giant
- Distance: 1 AU (middle of habitable zone)
- Atmosphere: Provides greenhouse warming, shields from radiation
- Magnetic field: Protects atmosphere from solar wind erosion
- Moon: Stabilizes axial tilt (prevents extreme climate variations)
- Plate tectonics: Recycles carbon, regulates temperature over billions of years

**Not all factors required for life, but helpful**:
- Could life exist on tidally locked planet? Possibly
- Could life exist without moon? Probably
- Could life exist without plate tectonics? Maybe
- But: Earth's combination is favorable

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Planet formation: Gravity (standing energy) organizing matter
- Habitable zone: Balance between stellar energy input (kinetic flux) and planetary temperature
- Atmosphere retention: Gravity (mass) vs. thermal velocity (temperature)
- Grammar: N-body dynamics + thermodynamics determine outcomes

### Step 4: Create Chemical Diversity

**Problem**: Simple molecules insufficient for life complexity

**Solution**: Heavy elements enable vast chemical space
- Carbon: Forms millions of compounds
  - Can bond to 4 other atoms (tetrahedral geometry)
  - Forms chains, rings, complex 3D structures
  - Essential for organic chemistry
- Oxygen: Highly reactive, forms bonds with most elements
  - Water (H₂O): Universal solvent
  - Enables oxidation-reduction reactions (energy transfer)
- Nitrogen: Essential for amino acids (proteins) and nucleic acids (DNA/RNA)
- Phosphorus: DNA/RNA backbone, ATP (energy currency)
- Sulfur: Protein structure (disulfide bonds), metabolic cofactors

**Chemical possibilities**:
- Known organic compounds: ~100 million (and growing)
- Potential organic compounds: Essentially infinite
- This diversity enables:
  - Catalysis (enzymes speed specific reactions)
  - Information storage (DNA, RNA sequences)
  - Energy conversion (metabolic pathways)
  - Structural materials (proteins, polysaccharides)

**Earth's early chemistry** (~4 billion years ago):
- Atmosphere: CO₂, N₂, H₂O, CH₄, NH₃ (no free O₂ yet)
- Energy sources: UV radiation, lightning, volcanic heat, impacts
- Synthesis: Simple organics form (amino acids, nucleotides, sugars)
- Concentration: Tidal pools, hydrothermal vents
- Polymerization: Monomers → polymers (proteins, nucleic acids)

**Verb-coin interpretation**:
- Chemical bonds: EM force (sharing/transferring electrons)
- Reactions: Face-0 (bonds) ↔ face-1 (energy released/absorbed)
- Catalysis: Lowering activation energy barriers
- Grammar: Chemical bonding rules from 3D molecular geometry

**Without chemical diversity**: No way to build complex self-replicating systems. No metabolism. No information storage. No life.
